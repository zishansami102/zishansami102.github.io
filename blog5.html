Random Tiltle

(random texts)Frankly, there is a lot of variance between the salary offered by companies among various sectors. I am talking numbers specific to the top 5 IIT's here. Finance companies takes the top spot in remuneration amount which can be an upward of 12lpa average.Such companies which include Goldman Sachs, Tower Research Capital, World Quant, each offering over 20lpa gross packages. Tech companies taking in computer science students also dole out huge salary packages typically in the range 10-20lpa. Google, Facebook, Sony come under this category. Then come the Consultancy firms which offer an average of 11-12lpa (Booz, Bain, McKinsey, etc). Of course there is a sporadic scatter of non-cs tech companies in the 10lpa+ category like Schlumberger. The remaining companies, including other tech, analytics, software development firms, etc. which actually comprise a huge chunk of the list, typically offer packages in the range of 4lpa-9lpa. That should pretty much give a top level idea about the placement scene in the top level IIT's. I must advice, DO NOT benchmark what you want to do in life based on such statistics. True, its good to have a well paying job, financial stability, etc etc. But it is more important to understand what you want to do early on by assessing what kind of activities you like, where your strengths lie, doing what makes you happy and then focus in getting better at that. Thats the toughest part, one has to do a lot of soul searching. But once you have something which you think is ultimately what you want to achieve say 10 years down the line, say 20, 30 years ahead. Put all your energy into doing that. This might or might not guarantee you riches, but as long as you are supremely happy in whatever it is that you are doing, I am sure success will follow. There goes an old saying - " What use hath a man with gold and silver for a heart which is not content"
